Overall Group

As a team, we found that we were at the very least consistent in with holding 2 meetings a week. We recognised also that some members held excellent skills and were better suited for certain roles and tasks. With this recognition, the task allocation was more efficient, playing to our member’s strengths rather than weaknesses. However, the team collectively identified that we have lots of aspects to improve on. A lack of participation and commitment were one of our biggest flaws that has led to several issues. This led to communication confusion within the team, lack of progress and eventually the necessity for a “crunch” period. Additionally, outstanding tasks for sections that were prioritised and conceptualised to be finished in the early stages of the assignment largely remained incomplete. In turn, the later stages of the assignment were also intervened by the need to address and complete said tasks.

Regarding the positives of the team dynamic, we have a diverse range of skills, from technical to non-technical. For instance, delegating Russell to the website design or having Bo manage the team environment allowed for a more optimised allocation of resources. The planning of tasks was smooth and practical, however, flaws in transparent communication and commitment from the collective proved to hinder our execution and consequently, the completion of the assignment in the later stages of the deadline made it more stressful than it needed to be. Therefore, as a team, more clarity needs to be addressed in communication as well as its frequency on teams. Further improvements could include incorporation of tools such as Trello when creating and assigning tasks, which also provides a supplementary system for progress management.

One aspect that the team found surprising would be the difficulty of operating in a team environment, especially an informal environment with people skilled in different areas. Making sure every member was up to speed and was working on their assigned tasks, with endeavours to reduce procrastination proved difficult to manage and caught many off guard with the amount of work left to be completed juxtaposed to the encroaching deadline. Interestingly, this point was a point of learning for the team. The efforts and enthusiasm of a team member can positively affect the whole team and contrastingly, the slack and last-minute attitude to a single member, has an adverse effect on the collective group, and often to a greater extent to its positive.

With the advent of the third and final assessment, we intend to make drastic changes to the groups structure and the way we perform and execute objectives from the lessons learned from this assignment.
